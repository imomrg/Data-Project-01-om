### \# 🧠 UNSUPERVISED LEARNING



\## 📖 What is Unsupervised Learning?



Unsupervised Learning is a type of Machine Learning where the model learns from \*\*unlabeled data\*\*.



It works \*\*without correct answers (labels)\*\*.



The model finds \*\*patterns, similarities, and relationships\*\* on its own.



\---



\## 🤔 Why is it called Unsupervised?



Because there is \*\*no teacher\*\* to guide the computer.



The computer has to learn by itself.



Think of it like this:



Teacher ❌



Questions + Answers ❌



Computer Learns by Itself ✅



\---



\## 🎯 Goal of Unsupervised Learning



\- Find hidden patterns

\- Group similar data

\- Discover relationships

\- Reduce unnecessary information



\---



\# 📂 Important Terms



\## Data



Information given to the computer.



Example



\- Age

\- Salary

\- Height

\- Weight



\---



\## Dataset



Collection of lots of data.



Example



Information of 5000 customers.



\---



\## Label



Correct Answer.



Example



Dog → Animal



Spam → Yes



Loan → Approved



In Unsupervised Learning,



❌ Labels are NOT available.



\---



\## Pattern



A relationship found by the computer.



Example



Customers with similar shopping habits.



\---



\## Cluster



A group of similar data.



Example



Group 1 → Students



Group 2 → Teachers



Group 3 → Doctors



The computer creates these groups automatically.



\---



\# ⚙️ Working of Unsupervised Learning



\## Step 1



Collect Unlabeled Data



Dataset without predefined labels.



Example



Pictures of animals without names.



↓



\## Step 2



Choose an Algorithm



Example



\- K-Means

\- DBSCAN

\- PCA



↓



\## Step 3



Train the Model



The computer studies all the data.



It searches for



\- Similarities

\- Relationships

\- Hidden Patterns



↓



\## Step 4



Group or Transform Data



The computer automatically creates groups.



Example



Cats together



Dogs together



Birds together



↓



\## Step 5



Interpret Results



Humans study the generated groups and use them for decision-making.



\---



\# 🔥 Types of Unsupervised Learning



\## 1. Clustering ⭐



Groups similar data together.



Example



Suppose an online shopping website has 10,000 customers.



The model automatically groups them into:



\- Students

\- Office Workers

\- Senior Citizens



without anyone telling it who belongs to which group.



\### Popular Clustering Algorithms



\- K-Means Clustering ⭐

\- Hierarchical Clustering

\- DBSCAN

\- Mean Shift



\### Applications



\- Customer Segmentation

\- Friend Suggestions

\- Image Grouping

\- Market Analysis



\---



\## 2. Association Rule Learning



Finds relationships between items.



Example



In a supermarket,



People who buy



Bread



also buy



Butter.



The computer discovers this pattern.



\### Popular Algorithms



\- Apriori Algorithm ⭐

\- FP-Growth



\### Applications



\- Amazon Product Recommendation

\- Market Basket Analysis

\- Online Shopping Suggestions



\---



\## 3. Dimensionality Reduction



Reduces unnecessary features while keeping important information.



Makes the dataset smaller and faster to process.



Example



Suppose a dataset has



100 Features.



Many are unnecessary.



The algorithm reduces them to



20 Important Features.



\### Popular Algorithms



\- PCA (Principal Component Analysis) ⭐

\- t-SNE

\- UMAP



\### Applications



\- Data Compression

\- Faster Training

\- Data Visualization

\# ✅ Advantages



✔ No labeled data required



✔ Finds hidden patterns



✔ Useful for large datasets



✔ Helps understand data better



✔ Can discover unknown relationships



\---



\# ❌ Disadvantages



✘ Accuracy is difficult to measure



✘ Results may be difficult to understand



✘ Different algorithms may produce different groups



✘ Sometimes creates incorrect clusters

\# 📊 Supervised vs Unsupervised



| Supervised                | Unsupervised          | 

|---------------------------|-----------------------|

| Uses Labeled Data         | Uses Unlabeled Data |

| Correct Answers Available | No Correct Answers |

| Predicts Output           | Finds Patterns |

| Teacher Available         | No Teacher |

| Loan Prediction           | Customer Segmentation |
